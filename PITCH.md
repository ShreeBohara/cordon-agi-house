# CORDON — Pitch & Demo Notes

**One-liner (project title / submission tagline):**
> CORDON — the immune system for AI agent swarms. It contains a prompt-injection outbreak before it becomes a breach.

---

## The 30-second pitch (12:15 lineup) — PRIMARY

Deliver this verbatim. ~30s at a brisk-but-calm pace. Emphasis on the *italics*.

> AI agents get their credentials at the door. The real problem is they get hijacked *after* they're inside — by a poisoned email — and in a swarm, that compromise spreads agent to agent, like a virus.
>
> **CORDON is the immune system for agent swarms.** The moment an agent touches something untrusted, it's cut off from the production key — the secret never even reaches the model. Then CORDON traces who it talked to and quarantines the *whole infected chain* in under a second.
>
> Identity says who an agent *is*. CORDON adds the axis nobody has built — who it *touched it with*. Come watch one poisoned email get contained.

*(~88 words. To hit a hard 28s, drop the final sentence.)*

## Delivery notes
- **Memorize the first sentence and the last sentence cold.** The middle can flex; the bookends can't.
- **Open on the problem, not the product.** "Agents get hijacked after they're inside" earns attention before you say a product name.
- **Land the closer slowly:** "who it *touched it with*" — pause — "come watch one poisoned email get contained." That's the line that pulls judges to your table.
- **Have the dashboard already open** on your laptop; if you can hold/angle the screen, the live graph behind you does free work.
- **Don't explain how it works** in 30s — that's the demo's job. The pitch sells the *problem* and the *hook*.
- Breathe at the paragraph breaks. Three beats: problem → what CORDON does → the memorable axis line.
